In a state of panic, they fled the premises, seizing whatever they could think of that they might need. There was no time to double-check that they were not leaving anything crucial behind. Everything was hurled into the automobile, and they sped away. Thirty minutes later, they were safe, and it was then that it dawned on them that they had forgotten the most important thing of all.

Monroe spoke to his partner over the phone. "I will discuss this with you in more detail at our meeting tomorrow, but I believe I have discovered a solution to our problem. It is not exactly legal, but it will not land us in jail for the rest of our lives either. Are you willing to take the risk?"

A furry humanoid seven feet tall, with the face of an intelligent gorilla and the braincase of a man, appeared in the sky. The canines would have been more appropriate for a tiger's face, and they protruded from the corners of their broad, thin-lipped mouths, giving them a fierce expression.

Kevin was a complex individual. No one truly understood him. It wasn't that he was particularly strange or difficult. It was more that there wasn't enough there that anyone wanted to take the time to comprehend him. This was a shame, as Kevin had many of the answers to the important questions that most people who knew him had. It was even more regrettable that they would refuse to listen even if Kevin offered to give them the answers. As a result, Kevin remained silent, misunderstood, and kept those important answers to life to himself.
